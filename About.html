About the Website

The author has observed in her school community that there are many types of learners. Three of the most common ones are: a). Visual Learners, who learn best with images; b). Verbal Learners who learn best by listening to tutorials; and c). Physical Learners who needs examples to perform their best. To accommodate these different types of learners, the author has created a website that provides the tools to help Learners study Mathematics in a more student-friendly way – quick, effective, and stress-free. The topics are taken from the International Baccalaureate (IB) school curriculum (Grades 9 – 10) and adapted into a modern media of study.
This website does not guarantee that learning Mathematics will be easy or can be done overnight as there is always a learning process behind. Tools of learning should be complemented with effort, to make the whole process successful. At the end, the author hopes this website will help to improve the Learners’ academic performance, especially in Mathematics.

About the Author

Michelle Alessandra Wardhojo is a Grade 10 student from Tunas Muda School who is passionate in Mathematics. During her learning journey, starting from 6th grade, she managed to obtain several awards in Mathematics Competitions internationally:
- Japan International Science and Mathematics Olympiad (JISMO) Medalist - Jakarta 2016
- World Mathematics Team Championship (WMTC) Medalist - Thailand 2017
- Southeast Asian Mathematics Olympiad (SEAMO - X) Medalist - Singapore 2019
- World Mathematics Invitational (WMI) Medalist - Japan 2019
- World Mathematics Invitational (WMI) Medalist - Online Competition 2020
- Completion of the Final Level of Kumon English Literature (2017) and Mathematics (2020)
Through these experiences, she finds Mathematics as a challenging, yet intriguing subject worth studying and exploring. She realizes that Mathematics is an important skill in life, that everyone should have, from simple addition and subtraction used in buying and selling, to Calculus used in Engineering.
